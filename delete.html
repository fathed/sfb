STT+ (TDMA) SIPR through NIPR

Add Title Information

  • Choose the appropriate internal connection from G0/10 on the NT2S to the interface going to the STT+.
    Once the data packet reaches the trunk port G0/51 of the NT2S, the packet will then be forwarded to the STT+ with the onboard AES router; for the packet to continue to the distant end.
  • Choose the appropriate connection from G0/0 to the NT2S.
    Since the destination of the packet is the AES router onboard the STT+, the router will analyze the routing table, locate the destination network, and reassign the packet to the G0/0.3 sub-interface (giving it a VLAN tag of 3) which is the network associated with the STT+. The data packet (with a VLAN tag of 3) will then enter trunk port G0/10 of the NT2S which allows VLAN 3 traffic.
  • Choose the appropriate connection from G0/10 of the NT2S to the NT2R.
    The data packet with the tag of VLAN 175 will be directed by the switch going to the sub-interface G0/0.175 on the NT2R. The router has a physical port of G0/0 where the traffic enters the router; within the physical port there are logical sub-interface assignments configured. The sub-interfaces of the router are the logical assignments of the different lanes of traffic that will traverse the network. When a sub-interface is configured inside the physical port of the router, the encapsulation statement determines the lane in which the packet will travel. The data packet with the tag of VLAN 175 enters G0/0, more specifically, G0/0.175, due to the VLAN ID 175 being assigned to the sub-interface.
  • Choose the appropriate internal connection from G0/12 of the NT2S to the trunk port connected to the NT2R.
    The data packet (with VLAN tag of 175) will be forwarded to trunk port G0/10 which allows VLAN 175 traffic.
  • Choose the appropriate connection from the SIPR Cloud (TACLANE#1) going to NT2S.
    The encrypted data packet (with VLAN tag of 175) will now enter G0/12 of the NT2S to continue its path to the STT+.
